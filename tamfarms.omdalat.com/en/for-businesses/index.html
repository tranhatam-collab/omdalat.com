For businesses

Businesses walking alongside the garden

Tam Farm can partner with businesses in several ways: sponsoring a residency for staff, sending employees to experience a new environment, collaborating on agriculture–technology projects, or introducing expert partners.

Forms of collaboration

Sponsor a residency

The business sponsors accommodation and living costs for one or more participants over a defined period. Suited to internal training programs, creative sabbaticals, or supporting staff through a transition.

Send staff for an experience

The business sends employees to live and work for 1–4 weeks to recharge, find new inspiration, or develop a creative project in a different environment.

Project collaboration

Businesses with agriculture, technology, education, or community projects that fit the garden model can run a trial together at a Tam Farm location.

Introduce experts

The business introduces experts, advisors, or partners to walk alongside participants in career orientation or project development programs.

Internal workshops

Host workshops, leadership retreats, or strategy working sessions in a quiet environment, close to nature, at a small scale.

Long-term membership

The business signs up for a membership so staff can rotate through experiences across the year, after a trial period and a fit assessment.

Note: Tam Farm does not guarantee recruitment, income, or ROI for businesses. The partnership is based on the value of experience and human development, not on an investment transaction that yields a return.
