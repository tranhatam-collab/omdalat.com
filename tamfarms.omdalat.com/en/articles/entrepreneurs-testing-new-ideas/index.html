For entrepreneurs who want to test new ideas and projects before investing big

A small testing environment for entrepreneurs, founders, and project teams to validate demand, products, and operations.

For: Entrepreneurs, founders, and project development teams

An idea can be very attractive on a business plan but fail when it meets real users, costs, and operating conditions.

Instead of investing big from the start, you can spend time shrinking the idea into an observable experiment.

What can be validated

  • Whether the user's problem is real.
  • Whether the product or service creates clear value.
  • Who is willing to use, pay, or partner.
  • The minimum cost and resources to operate.
  • Whether the founding team can work together.

A small project with evidence

In one to three months, the goal is not necessarily to incorporate right away. The goal can be:

  • A prototype.
  • A landing page.
  • A test user group.
  • A service process.
  • A testing report.
  • A timely decision to stop.

Connecting with agriculture, technology, and community

Depending on the project and location conditions, participants can observe real needs in agriculture, education, responsible tourism, technology, or the local community.

There is no commitment to investment, fundraising, profit, or project intake.

A good test doesn't just prove an idea is right. It also helps you discover early when an idea isn't good enough to keep investing in.

Estimated experience duration

Minimum 1 week, maximum 3 months. Limited intake each month — register in advance to discuss fit, availability, cost, and specific conditions.
