## 2. Roles

- Guest
- Applicant
- Resident
- Contributor
- Mentor
- Project Owner
- Operator
- Legal Reviewer
- Admin
- Founder Auditor

Users may hold multiple scoped roles. A global role does not automatically grant access to restricted Lily data.

## 3. Dashboard cards

### Applicant
Application status, missing actions, messages, interview, offer and secure document request.

### Resident
Stay term, room, weekly review, calendar, house rules, programmes, tasks, support and incident reporting.

### Contributor
Assignments, deadlines, submissions, feedback, compensation status and portfolio permissions.

### Mentor
Learners, submissions, review queue, checkpoints and notes.

### Operator
Room occupancy, arrivals/departures, application queue, incidents, maintenance and temporary-residence status.

### Admin
All release states, overrides, access logs, audit events, configuration and publication controls.

## 4. Role constraints

- Applicant cannot see other applicants or room residents.
- Resident cannot see another resident’s documents, payment or review.
- Mentor sees only assigned learners and approved fields.
- Project owner sees task-relevant data, not immigration documents.
- Operator sees stay operations; passport access is separately permissioned.
- Legal reviewer sees assigned legal cases and evidence.
- Admin overrides require reason and audit event.
- Founder Auditor is read-only by default.

## 5. Notifications

- application received;
- action/document requested;
- interview scheduled;
- offer sent/expiring;
- payment confirmed;
- arrival checklist;
- weekly review;
- task feedback;
- visa/status expiry warning;
- incident update;
- extension decision.

Restricted data is never placed in email subjects, push previews or analytics labels.

## 6. Workspace UX rules

- Each state has a clear next action.
- No empty dashboard without orientation.
- “Work” is hidden or locked when eligibility is not met.
- Legal status shows facts and expiry, not legal conclusions generated by AI.
- All critical actions have confirmation and undo/appeal route where appropriate.
- Mobile is the approval and resident-control surface, not a reduced marketing page.

## 7. Admin release control

Public Lily content is drafted in CMS, reviewed and promoted only through:

```text
draft → content_review → legal/compliance_review → admin_approved → published
```

Rollback restores the previous approved public revision and records the event.
