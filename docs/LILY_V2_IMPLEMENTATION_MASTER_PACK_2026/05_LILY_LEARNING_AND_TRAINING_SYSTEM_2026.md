# LILY LEARNING AND TRAINING SYSTEM 2026

## 1. Learning model

Lily learning follows:

```text
Read / Watch
→ Understand
→ Practice
→ Submit
→ Review
→ Revise
→ Pass
→ Apply to a verified project when eligible
```

A course is not considered live unless it has learning objectives, content, assignment, rubric, pass threshold, feedback path and responsible reviewer.

## 2. Track catalogue

### A. Digital Foundation
- Duration: 1–2 weeks
- Goal: basic digital safety, files, cloud tools, communication and documentation.
- Output: personal work system and security checklist.
- Evaluation: 70% rubric and completed safety check.

### B. AI Work Basics
- Duration: 1–2 weeks
- Goal: prompt structure, verification, source handling, privacy and responsible AI use.
- Output: one reusable AI-assisted workflow with human verification.
- Evaluation: accuracy, transparency, privacy and repeatability.

### C. Content Operations
- Duration: 2–4 weeks
- Goal: research, outline, bilingual editorial process, CMS and QA.
- Output: approved article/content package.
- Evaluation: clarity, evidence, originality, brand language and CMS quality.

### D. SEO Operations
- Duration: 2–4 weeks
- Goal: search intent, on-page SEO, internal linking, schema, local SEO and reporting.
- Output: SEO brief and implemented page audit.
- Evaluation: technical completeness, relevance and no overclaim.

### E. Brand Factory Operations
- Duration: 3–6 weeks
- Goal: intake, verification, story, image rights, bilingual copy, compliance and release.
- Output: private-preview local brand profile.
- Evaluation: truth, evidence, consent, usability and release readiness.

### F. Remote Collaboration
- Duration: 1–2 weeks
- Goal: briefs, async updates, deadlines, versioning, handoff and professional conduct.
- Output: completed simulated or real team task.
- Evaluation: communication, timeliness, proof and handoff quality.

### G. Project Builder
- Duration: 4–12 weeks
- Goal: define, build, test and present a practical digital project.
- Output: working artefact plus evidence pack.
- Evaluation: user need, implementation, QA, documentation and responsible claims.

## 3. Lesson data contract

Required fields:

```text
lesson_id, track_id, title_vi, title_en, objective_vi, objective_en,
content_blocks, assignment, rubric, pass_threshold, retry_limit,
required_previous_lessons, reviewer_role, safety_notes, publication_status
```

## 4. Assessment states

- `not_started`
- `in_progress`
- `submitted`
- `revision_required`
- `passed`
- `escalated_review`
- `waived_with_reason`

## 5. Mentor intervention

Mentors handle:

- repeated failure;
- ambiguous evaluation;
- strategic product review;
- sensitive legal/financial claims;
- final builder review.

AI may draft feedback or detect missing fields but cannot issue final pass for sensitive or high-impact submissions.

## 6. Programme enrolment

Stay does not automatically enrol a resident. Programme participation requires:

- resident/applicant eligibility;
- capacity;
- accepted terms;
- programme owner;
- fee status;
- required device/access;
- language suitability;
- legal work distinction.

## 7. Completion proof

Certificates or badges must state exactly what was completed. They do not claim employment, professional licensing, visa eligibility or guaranteed competence outside the assessed scope.
