# LILY PRIVACY, TERMS AND COMPLIANCE STANDARD 2026

**Important:** This document is an implementation standard, not final legal advice. Public terms and operational procedures require qualified review before production.

## 1. Separate agreements

1. Accommodation agreement
2. Programme/learning terms
3. Task/project agreement
4. International-support/referral consent
5. Media/case-study consent

One agreement or consent does not authorise all purposes.

## 2. Data classification

### Public
Approved Lily pages, verified facilities, approved images and public programmes.

### Internal
Application status, non-sensitive notes, calendar and programme progress.

### Confidential
Agreements, payment, task feedback, complaints and incident data.

### Restricted
Passport, visa, immigration, health/accessibility, criminal-record, permit/exemption and sensitive financial data.

## 3. Processing principles

- purpose limitation;
- data minimisation;
- informed and separable consent where consent is relied on;
- access control and encryption;
- retention schedule;
- correction, withdrawal and deletion workflow subject to legal retention;
- incident response;
- cross-border and third-party review;
- no sale of personal data;
- no AI/model training on resident data without explicit, specific approval.

The system must be reviewed against current Vietnamese personal-data law, including Decree 13/2023/NĐ-CP and any successor or amendment.

## 4. Passport handling

- Public form never accepts passport files.
- Upload requires authenticated session and explicit request.
- Files use restricted storage, encryption and short-lived access links.
- Access is limited to assigned operator/legal reviewer roles.
- Every view/download is logged.
- Copies are retained only for documented operational/legal need.
- Passport data never appears in analytics, search, notifications or content systems.

Quiet hours, guests, smoking/alcohol/substances, pets, kitchen, laundry, cleaning, utilities, garden tools/chemicals, waste, damage, keys/access, photography, harassment/discrimination, emergencies and serious-breach termination.

## 7. Safety register

- fire/extinguisher/evacuation evidence;
- electrical and power-load checks;
- water and sanitation;
- first aid;
- slips, paths and garden terrain;
- tools and chemicals;
- animal/insect risks;
- severe weather;
- medical emergency;
- incident log and corrective action.

## 8. International disclaimers

- Lily is not an immigration authority.
- Information may become outdated and must be revalidated.
- Admission does not grant work rights.
- A visa/stay status does not automatically permit local work.
- Sponsorship or permit support is never guaranteed.
- Third-party advisers have separate terms and fees.

## 9. Incident workflow

```text
reported
→ immediate safety action
→ access/data containment where needed
→ operator assigned
→ evidence preserved
→ affected person informed as appropriate
→ investigation
→ corrective action
→ resolved or escalated
→ post-incident review
```

## 10. Retention schedule baseline

Exact periods require legal review. Configure by class and purpose, not “keep forever”. Suggested design:

- rejected/incomplete applications: short operational retention;
- resident agreements/payment records: statutory/contractual retention;
- passport/visa copies: minimum necessary period;
- incidents/disputes: retention based on limitation and safety need;
- marketing consent: until withdrawal plus suppression evidence;
- audit logs: security/compliance period with restricted access.
