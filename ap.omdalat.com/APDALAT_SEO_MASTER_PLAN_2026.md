## 0. Executive Lock

`ap.omdalat.com` is the independent editorial layer for Đà Lạt. It exists to help people read, see, understand, and feel Đà Lạt as a real living place.

It is not:

- a tourism site
- a hotel, stay, or tour directory
- a generic lifestyle magazine
- a pre-landing page for `omdalat.com`
- a duplicate SEO surface for Om Dalat

The relationship is:

- `ap.omdalat.com` = understand, read, see, feel, notice
- `omdalat.com` = stay, work, learn, join, take part

All SEO, content, UX/UI, schema, sitemap, and metadata decisions must preserve this separation.

## 2. SEO Objective

Ap Dalat SEO must:

- build authority for contemporary Đà Lạt as a lived place
- rank for editorial discovery intents that Om Dalat should not own
- create durable topical clusters around people, places, rhythms, work, stories, and images
- keep Vietnamese-first language quality even when optimizing for search
- pass qualified readers to Om Dalat only when their intent becomes deeper participation
- avoid tourism keywords that would attract the wrong behavior

SEO success is not measured by traffic alone. The site must attract readers who care about Đà Lạt beyond passing through.

## 5. Vietnamese-First Title And Meta Rules

Vietnamese is the source language. English is not a shortened or mechanical translation.

### 5.1 Title Rules

Hard rules:

- 45-65 characters target when possible
- one primary idea only
- natural Vietnamese first, keyword second
- no clickbait
- no exclamation marks
- no tourism phrasing such as "check-in", "must visit", "top places", "hidden gem", "tour", "travel guide"
- include `Ấp Đà Lạt` only when brand context matters
- category and article titles must not duplicate homepage title logic

Preferred structures:

- `[Subject] | [human/editorial angle]`
- `[Story title] | Ấp Đà Lạt`
- `[Category] Đà Lạt | [specific lived angle]`

Approved homepage:

- VI title: `Ấp Đà Lạt | Những con người, nơi chốn và nhịp sống Đà Lạt`
- EN title: `Ap Dalat | People, Places and Rhythms of Dalat`

### 5.2 Meta Description Rules

Hard rules:

- 120-155 characters target when possible
- describe the actual page, not the brand dream
- include a lived detail when natural
- no generic SEO filler
- no "best", "top", "guide", "destination", "experience" unless the page truly requires it and editorial approves
- no duplicated meta across routes

Approved homepage:

- VI meta: `Hiểu Đà Lạt như một nơi để ở lại, làm việc và giữ một nhịp sống có thể đi đường dài.`
- EN meta: `Read Dalat as a living place through its people, places, work, and daily rhythms.`

### 5.3 H1 Rules

- exactly one H1 per page
- H1 can be more editorial than the title
- H1 must not be a keyword list
- homepage H1 is locked by the homepage copy master

Approved homepage H1:

- VI: `Những con người, nơi chốn và nhịp sống làm nên Đà Lạt hôm nay.`
- EN: `People, places, and rhythms shaping Dalat today.`

### 7.2 Article Minimum Links

Each article must include:

- one link to its parent category
- one link to a same-cluster article
- one link to a different but relevant cluster
- one language alternate when translated
- one Om Dalat bridge only if the article reaches a valid stay/work/join intent

### 7.3 Cluster Rules

People cluster links:

- link between profiles by shared theme, not random author/date
- link to Work when the person story includes craft, online work, or staying to build something
- bridge to Om only when the reader could reasonably want to participate

Places cluster links:

- link from place essay to rhythm or image essay with the same atmosphere
- avoid turning place pages into location listings
- bridge to Om only when the page is about staying or working longer

Rhythms cluster links:

- link to stories and images
- use Work links only when rhythm affects actual working life
- bridge rarely

Work cluster links:

- link to people, places, and practical work essays
- allow a final bridge to Om Dalat when the page points beyond reading
- never use "Apply now" on Ap

Images cluster links:

- link to the story/place/rhythm that gives the image context
- no forced Om bridge
- captions must carry editorial meaning, not tourism hooks

## 9. Schema Requirements

### 9.1 Sitewide Schema

Required on homepage:

- `Organization`
- `WebSite`
- `WebPage`

Organization fields:

- `name`: `Ap Dalat`
- `alternateName`: `Ấp Đà Lạt`
- `url`: `https://ap.omdalat.com`
- `sameAs`: only approved official profiles

WebSite fields:

- `name`: locale-specific brand name
- `url`: canonical host
- `inLanguage`: `vi-VN` or `en`
- `potentialAction`: SearchAction only if internal search is stable and noindex search pages are handled correctly

### 9.2 Category Schema

Use:

- `CollectionPage`
- `BreadcrumbList`

Each category page must declare:

- canonical URL
- language
- page name
- description
- breadcrumb path

### 9.3 Article Schema

Use `Article` or `BlogPosting` consistently. Required fields:

- `headline`
- `description`
- `image`
- `author`
- `publisher`
- `datePublished`
- `dateModified`
- `inLanguage`
- `mainEntityOfPage`
- `articleSection`
- `keywords` only if curated, not stuffed

### 9.4 Image Schema

Use `ImageObject` for image essays and hero images when useful:

- `contentUrl`
- `caption`
- `description`
- `creator` or source when known
- `license` when applicable
- `width`
- `height`

### 9.5 FAQ Schema

Use `FAQPage` only for the FAQ page or genuine FAQ blocks. Do not add FAQ schema to normal essays just to seek rich results.

## 10. Indexation Rules

### 10.1 Index

Index only production public HTML pages that are complete:

- homepage
- localized category pages
- localized article pages
- about / Om Ap Dalat
- FAQ
- contact
- image essays with completed alt/caption/source

### 10.2 Noindex Or Exclude

Noindex or disallow:

- `/tim-kiem/`
- `/en/search/`
- `/assets/`
- `/content/`
- `/docs/`
- `/images/excluded/`
- preview URLs
- draft pages
- broken locale variants
- pages missing title/meta/canonical
- pages with placeholder text
- English pages that are not editorial-quality yet

### 10.3 Canonical

- canonical base is always `https://ap.omdalat.com`
- each locale canonical points to itself
- EN must not canonical to VI
- Vercel or preview aliases must never appear in canonical, sitemap, hreflang, OG, or schema
- old or duplicate paths must 301/308 to the canonical path when consolidated

### 10.4 Sitemap

Maintain the phase 2 sitemap set:

- `sitemap.xml`
- `sitemap-pages.xml`
- `sitemap-stories.xml`
- `sitemap-images.xml`
- `sitemap-vi.xml`
- `sitemap-en.xml`

Whenever a static route is added, removed, or renamed, update the sitemap in the same change.

## 11. Bilingual SEO Rules

### 11.1 Locale Architecture

- Vietnamese default: `/`
- English default: `/en/`
- `x-default` should point to the Vietnamese homepage unless a language selector page is introduced
- use `vi-VN` for Vietnamese
- use `en` for English

### 11.2 Hreflang

Every translated pair must include reciprocal hreflang:

- VI page references EN alternate
- EN page references VI alternate
- both include self-reference
- homepage includes `x-default`

Do not create hreflang for missing, thin, draft, or weak translations.

### 11.3 Translation Quality

English pages must:

- read as native editorial English
- keep the same intent as the Vietnamese source
- avoid flattening emotional detail
- use `Dalat`, not inconsistent spellings
- use `Ap Dalat`, not `Ấp Đà Lạt`, when English readability requires the English display name

Vietnamese pages remain the source of meaning. English can adapt rhythm and wording, but cannot change the page intent.

## 12. Image SEO And Alt Matrix

Every image used on an indexable page must have:

- descriptive alt text in the page language
- caption when the image is editorial, documentary, or gallery content
- human-readable filename
- width and height
- responsive source where applicable
- optimized format
- no watermarked, broken, placeholder, or excluded source
- clear source/rights status

Alt text must describe what is visible and meaningful:

- good: `Một bàn làm việc nhỏ nhìn ra khu vườn trong buổi sáng nhiều sương ở Đà Lạt`
- good: `A narrow hillside lane in Dalat after rain, with small homes along the slope`
- bad: `ảnh đẹp Đà Lạt`
- bad: `best Dalat place`
- bad: `Dalat travel check-in view`

The image metadata and alt evidence must be entered in the Ap route and metadata matrix before launch review.

Do not publish SEO copy, metadata, headings, alt text, captions, or schema that frames Ap Dalat as:

- "địa điểm du lịch"
- "top địa điểm"
- "check-in"
- "must visit"
- "hidden gem"
- "tour guide"
- "travel guide"
- "best cafes in Dalat"
- "things to do in Dalat"
- "Dalat itinerary"
- "destination"
- "bucket list"

Allowed alternatives:

- nơi chốn có người ở lại
- một góc làm việc nhỏ
- nhịp sống buổi sáng
- câu chuyện của người ở lại
- một vùng sống
- a lived place
- a working rhythm
- people and places shaping Dalat
- a visual essay from Dalat

If a keyword is high-volume but pulls tourism intent, reject it unless the article explicitly reframes the intent without baiting tourism traffic.

## 14. Cross-Site Bridge Rules

Ap may link to Om only when the reader's intent has moved from reading to deeper action.

Allowed bridge contexts:

- end of a work article
- end of a story about staying longer
- About page
- Om Ap Dalat page
- homepage near-bottom bridge block
- category footer for Work when phrased softly

Blocked bridge contexts:

- article hero
- first paragraph
- every article by default
- visual gallery middle
- thumbnail cards
- repeated banners
- generic footer spam

Approved bridge language:

- VI: `Nếu việc đọc không còn là đủ, hãy tiếp tục tại Ôm Đà Lạt.`
- VI: `Nếu bạn muốn ở lại sâu hơn, hãy đi tới Ôm Đà Lạt.`
- EN: `If reading is no longer enough, continue with Om Dalat.`
- EN: `If you are looking for a deeper way to stay and take part, continue to Om Dalat.`

Forbidden bridge language:

- `Tham gia ngay`
- `Bắt đầu hành trình`
- `Đăng ký để đổi đời`
- `Join now`
- `Start your journey today`
- `Transform your life`
- `Unlock the lifestyle`

## 16. Content Publishing Workflow

Before writing:

1. Pick one keyword territory.
2. Assign one page intent.
3. Confirm Ap, not Om, should own that intent.
4. Pick cluster and internal-link targets.
5. Confirm no tourism bait.

Before publishing:

1. Vietnamese draft passes language lock.
2. English version exists only if it is publish-quality.
3. Title/meta/H1 are unique.
4. Images have alt/caption/filename/source status.
5. Internal links are added.
6. Schema fields are complete.
7. Sitemap and hreflang are updated.
8. Matrix evidence path is filled.

After publishing:

1. Fetch the production URL.
2. Confirm 200 status.
3. Confirm canonical, hreflang, metadata, OG, schema.
4. Confirm no search/disallowed raw paths leak.
5. Submit sitemap only after production smoke passes.
