## Status: Locked

## Required by: Founder / Design / Content / Frontend / SEO / CMS / Editorial Ops
## Purpose: Define how Ấp Đà Lạt uses images so the visual system remains truthful, atmospheric, legally safe, and editorial rather than touristic
## System lock reference: `docs/OMDALAT_AND_APDALAT_IMAGE_REALITY_STANDARD_2026.md`

---

# 0. IMAGE LOCK STATEMENT

On Ấp Đà Lạt, images are not decoration.
They are evidence of:
light,
texture,
weather,
work,
daily life,
materials,
and the human scale of Dalat.

If the wrong image system is used, the brand will immediately drift into:
tourism,
hotel aesthetics,
generic lifestyle,
or stock-photo emptiness.

This file is the source of truth for image sourcing, licensing, selection, cropping, captions, galleries, performance, and QA.

---

# 1. VISUAL PURPOSE

The image system must help readers feel:

1. This is a real place
2. This place is inhabited
3. Beauty here comes from life, not staging
4. Work and living are part of the same visual world
5. Dalat is more than postcard scenery

---

# 2. IMAGE PRINCIPLES

All public images should lean toward:
real
quiet
textured
atmospheric
human
light-sensitive
weather-aware
material
specific
editorial

Avoid images that feel:
tourism-forward
luxury hospitality
over-produced
generic remote-work fantasy
hyper-saturated
only scenic with no life
staged for sales

# 4. SOURCE POLICY

## 4.1 Preferred source order

1. Original commissioned or owned images
2. Real contributor images with permission
3. Open-license sources with verified usage rights
4. Archive or public-domain material where appropriate and lawful

## 4.2 Approved external source classes

Can use approved open-license or permissive-use sources only if:
the license is checked
the editorial fit is strong
the image does not feel generic
required credit conditions are followed

## 4.3 Do not use

Images scraped from random websites
Pinterest origin-unknown images
Instagram images without permission
watermarked content
AI-generated pseudo-Dalat visuals for editorial truth surfaces
hotel marketing images unless the story explicitly analyzes that subject

---

# 5. ORIGINAL IMAGE PRIORITY RULE

Whenever possible, prioritize real Dalat-specific imagery that includes:
actual weather
actual materials
actual local environments
actual people
actual work settings

The more the site uses original or clearly place-specific imagery, the stronger the brand moat becomes.

---

# 6. VISUAL STYLE GUIDELINES

## 6.1 Desired visual qualities

soft natural light
mist or cool air where true
texture-rich surfaces
human-scale framing
honest color
quiet movement
evidence of use
depth without spectacle

## 6.2 Avoid these visual mistakes

over-saturated greens
orange-and-teal grading
fake cinematic fog
hard luxury interior styling
generic digital nomad laptop shot
symmetry for its own sake
empty scenic shots with no human trace
travel influencer poses

---

# 7. IMAGE SELECTION RULES

Choose an image only if at least 4 of these are true:

1. It feels real
2. It belongs to Dalat or plausibly supports a Dalat story
3. It adds atmosphere, not just prettiness
4. It supports the article angle
5. It is not generic tourism language in image form
6. It has strong composition
7. It preserves quietness
8. It can crop well for desktop and mobile

---

# 8. HUMAN IMAGE RULES

If a person’s face is visible:
use only images with permission or lawful editorial use
avoid exploitative framing
avoid making ordinary people look like ad models
respect dignity
avoid over-stylized editing

Portraits should feel:
calm
present
real
specific
not over-directed

---

# 9. WORK IMAGE RULES

On Ấp Đà Lạt, work must look real.

Good work images:
desk with use
tools with wear
hands working
studio process
kitchen prep
editing corner
writing table
making, teaching, growing, fixing

Bad work images:
generic coworking stock
beach laptop fantasy
performative startup teamwork
posed “remote lifestyle” cliché

---

# 10. PLACE IMAGE RULES

Place imagery should help answer:
What does it feel like here
How is it used
Can someone live or work here
What texture or rhythm does it hold

Do not turn place pages into:
property listing galleries
resort showcases
“best view” competitions

---

# 11. GALLERY SYSTEM

## 11.1 Gallery types

Type A:
support gallery inside article
2 to 5 images

Type B:
place gallery
4 to 10 images

Type C:
photo essay gallery
6 to 20 images

Type D:
homepage editorial strip
3 to 6 images

## 11.2 Gallery principles

order matters
sequence should feel intentional
images should carry rhythm
avoid near-duplicates
mix wide, medium, and detail shots
let the set breathe

## 11.3 Gallery UX rules

clean navigation
easy swipe on mobile
visible captions if available
no over-engineered carousel behavior
no auto-play
no aggressive zoom gimmicks

---

# 12. HERO IMAGE RULES

Every major page should have a hero image that:
sets mood quickly
supports the page thesis
crops well across devices
does not require text overlay to work
is strong enough to carry first impression

## 12.1 Hero mistakes to avoid

too busy
overly dark with unreadable overlay
beautiful but irrelevant
too generic
obviously stock-travel feeling
lacking depth
lacking visual calm

---

# 13. CROP RULES

Every important image should support:
desktop hero crop
mobile hero crop
card crop
Open Graph crop

## 13.1 Crop principles

protect the subject
protect negative space when needed
avoid losing essential light or atmosphere
do not crop a place so tightly that it becomes generic
do not crop a person awkwardly for mobile

---

# 14. FILE NAMING RULES

All assets should use descriptive, clean names.

Good examples:
dalat-rainy-morning-work-table.jpg
trai-mat-window-fog-01.webp
small-porch-dalat-evening-light.jpg

Bad examples:
IMG_3489.jpg
final-final-new.jpg
beautiful-dalat-best-view.jpg

---

# 15. CAPTION POLICY

Captions should add:
meaning
context
rhythm
atmosphere
use

They should not merely label what is visible.

Good caption:
Một hiên nhà nhỏ giữ lại khoảng nghỉ giữa công việc và mưa chiều.

Bad caption:
Beautiful porch in Dalat.

---

# 16. ALT TEXT POLICY

Alt text must support accessibility first.
It should:
describe the image clearly
stay concise
avoid keyword stuffing
reflect relevant context when useful

Good alt:
Một bàn làm việc nhỏ cạnh cửa sổ trong buổi sáng mưa ở Đà Lạt

Bad alt:
Đà Lạt đẹp work remote life inspiring photo

---

# 17. CREDIT AND LICENSE POLICY

Every externally sourced image must have:
source record
license note
credit requirement note if applicable
editorial approval record

If any of those are missing, do not publish the image.

Maintain an internal asset log.

---

# 18. AI IMAGE POLICY

AI-generated images must not be used to fake real Dalat editorial reality.

Can use AI images only for:
internal moodboards
concept exploration
non-editorial placeholder work before launch
explicitly labeled illustration surfaces if ever approved later

Do not use AI-generated “Dalat-like” editorial images on public story pages.

---

# 19. IMAGE PERFORMANCE RULES

Because the site is image-led, performance discipline is mandatory.

Must support:
responsive image sizes
modern formats where appropriate
lazy loading below fold
compressed versions
mobile-first delivery
low layout shift
optimized thumbnails

Avoid:
uploading full-resolution originals to live
uncontrolled gallery payloads
heavy scripts just for image effects

---

# 20. HOMEPAGE IMAGE MIX RULE

Homepage should not be all landscape.

Recommended mix:
1 strong atmospheric hero
2 people or livelihood images
2 place/detail images
1 work-related image
1 visual essay entry

This prevents postcard drift.

---

# 21. PHOTO ESSAY RULES

A photo essay should:
have a clear theme
progress with intention
balance wide shots and details
use restrained text
feel like a visual reading experience

Possible themes:
morning tables
porches
windows in rain
greenhouse lights
work corners
small kitchens
edge-of-city gardens
light through cold air

---

# 22. ARTICLE IMAGE RULES

## 22.1 Standard article

1 hero image
0 to 4 inline images if needed

## 22.2 People profile

1 portrait hero
2 to 6 supporting images

## 22.3 Place page

1 place hero
4 to 10 gallery images

## 22.4 Rhythm note

1 strong hero may be enough
optional 1 to 2 supporting images

## 22.5 Work story

1 work or place hero
2 to 4 detail/support images

---

# 23. DESIGN QA FOR IMAGES

Before publishing, verify:

1. Does the image feel real
2. Does it fit the article
3. Does it avoid travel cliché
4. Is the crop strong on mobile
5. Is the file optimized
6. Is alt text complete
7. Is caption useful
8. Is the license known
9. Does the gallery sequence feel intentional
10. Would this image still strengthen the brand without text

---

# 24. MVP IMAGE ASSET REQUIREMENTS

Before launch, prepare at minimum:
1 homepage hero set
6 category hero images
10 article hero images
5 people portrait sets
5 place gallery sets
3 image essay sets
Open Graph image variants
desktop + mobile crops for hero assets

---

# 25. DEFINITION OF DONE

The image and gallery system is complete when:
the site looks editorial,
feels place-specific,
avoids tourism clichés,
supports reading and memory,
and remains fast and legally safe.

---

# 26. FINAL IMAGE LOCK

On Ấp Đà Lạt, beauty must come from truth, texture, light, and use.
If the image system becomes generic, the brand becomes generic with it.
