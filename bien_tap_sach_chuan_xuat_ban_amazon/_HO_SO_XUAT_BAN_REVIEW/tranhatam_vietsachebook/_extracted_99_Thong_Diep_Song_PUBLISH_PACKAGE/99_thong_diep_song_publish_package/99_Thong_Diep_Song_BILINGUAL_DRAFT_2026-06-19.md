# 99 THONG DIEP SONG / 99 MESSAGES FOR LIVING
## Nhung nhat cat vao dieu tuong da hieu / Cuts into what seems already understood
**Tran Ha Tam / Tran Ha Tam**

---

## Loi mo / Opening

VI: Cuon sach nay khong dung ve phia loi khuyen. Khong dung ve phia an ui. Khong dung ve phia mot he thong giai thich doi song. Chin muoi chin cau trong sach khong duoc viet de huong dan phai song the nao. Chung chi di vao mot viec kho hon nhieu: cat vao dieu tuong nhu da hieu roi.
EN: This book does not stand on the side of advice. It does not stand on the side of comfort. It does not stand on the side of a system that explains life. The ninety-nine sentences in this book were not written to instruct how one must live. They only go into something far more difficult: a cut into what seems already understood.

VI: Moi cau can duoc doc cham, mot minh, trong khoang lang du sach de khong bi bien thanh khau hieu. Neu co cau nao cham trung, dung voi gat dau. Neu co cau nao lam kho chiu, dung voi chong lai. Co the chinh o noi nguoi doc muon di qua nhanh nhat, mot lop ngon ngu cu dang bat dau roi xuong.
EN: Each sentence should be read slowly, alone, in a silence clean enough that it does not turn into a slogan. If a sentence strikes, do not rush to nod. If a sentence irritates, do not rush to resist. Perhaps precisely where the reader wants to pass most quickly, an old layer of language is beginning to fall away.

VI: Day khong phai sach giai nghia con so. Khong phai ban do thanh cong. Khong phai cam nang chua lanh. Day la mot vat doc ngan nhung khong nhe. Doc xong, dieu con lai khong nen la mot ket luan. Dieu con lai nen la mot do im khac.
EN: This is not a book that interprets numbers. It is not a map to success. It is not a manual for healing. It is a short read, but not a light one. After reading, what remains should not be a conclusion. What remains should be a different quality of stillness.

## Cach doc cuon sach nay / How to read this book

VI: Co the doc tu dau den cuoi. Cung co the mo ngau nhien mot cau, dung lai, va dong sach.
EN: You may read from beginning to end. You may also open to a random sentence, stop, and close the book.

VI: Khong can co hieu du chin muoi chin cau trong mot lan. Mot cau dung luc co the du cho mot ngay. Mot vong dung luc co the du cho mot quang dai hon.
EN: You do not need to understand all ninety-nine sentences at once. One sentence at the right time may be enough for a day. One circle at the right time may be enough for a longer stretch.

VI: Neu muon doc theo nhip sach, hay di tu Vong 1 den Vong 9. Neu muon doc theo truc giac, hay de cuon sach tu mo o noi no can mo.
EN: If you wish to follow the rhythm of the book, go from Circle 1 to Circle 9. If you wish to read by intuition, let the book open where it needs to open.

# Vong 1 / Circle 1 — Truoc ca so / Before numbers

VI: Moi su bat dau deu dien ra truoc khi duoc danh so. Phan dau tien cua cuon sach nay di vao noi cac ten goi chua kip on dinh, noi dieu tuong la nen mong hoa ra van dang rung chuyen. Doc cham. Dung co hieu nhanh. Nhung cau o day khong giai thich su khoi dau. Chung chi lam lo ra rang nhieu dieu tung duoc xem la diem xuat phat thuc ra chi la lop phu duoc dat len sau cung.
EN: Every beginning takes place before it is numbered. The first part of this book enters where names have not yet settled, where what seemed to be the foundation is still trembling. Read slowly. Do not try to understand quickly. The sentences here do not explain the beginning. They only reveal that many things once seen as the starting point were in fact a layer placed on top, last.

# Vong 2 / Circle 2 — Hanh dong khong thay minh / Action that does not see itself

VI: Khi hanh dong khong con dung ra tu ke cong, nhung gi am tham hon bat dau lo dien. Vong nay di vao do tre, su ne tranh, thoi quen lap lai, va nhung quyet dinh da xong tu lau ma mieng van chua chiu nhan. Khong can tim loi khuyen. Chi can nhin xem phan nao trong doi song dang van hanh ma khong can duoc thay.
EN: When action no longer stands forth to claim credit, what is quieter begins to appear. This circle enters delay, avoidance, repeating habits, and decisions that were finished long ago while the mouth still refuses to admit it. No advice is needed. Only look at which part of life is operating without needing to be seen.

# Vong 3 / Circle 3 — Ten cua thoi gian / The name of time

VI: Thoi gian khong chi troi. No doi ten moi thu. Vong nay cham vao cai muon, cai som, cai chua toi, cai da qua, va nhung cach con nguoi dung ngon ngu de giu lay dieu von khong dung yen. Nhung cau o day khong do thoi gian. Chung cat vao cach thoi gian dang duoc goi ten.
EN: Time does not only flow. It renames everything. This circle touches what is late, what is early, what has not yet arrived, what has passed, and the ways people use language to hold onto what never stands still. The sentences here do not measure time. They cut into how time is being named.

# Vong 4 / Circle 4 — Than the da biet / What the body already knows

VI: Co nhung dieu than the biet truoc khi mieng kip noi va dau oc kip hieu. Vong nay tra chu nghia ve cham hon da, lung, tay, hoi tho, va vet thuong. Doc bang than the, khong chi bang suy nghi. Neu co mot phan trong ban phan ung truoc khi ban kip dong y hay phan doi, hay tin vao phan ung do.
EN: There are things the body knows before the mouth can speak and the mind can understand. This circle returns language to something slower than skin, back, hand, breath, and wound. Read with the body, not only with thought. If there is a part of you that reacts before you can agree or disagree, trust that reaction.

# Vong 5 / Circle 5 — Khoang cach / Distance

VI: Moi quan he deu duoc viet bang khoang cach: gan, xa, o lai, roi di, im lang, cho doi. Vong nay khong phan tich tinh cam. No di thang vao cau truc vo hinh giua nguoi voi nguoi. Co nhung cho o gan ma khong that. Co nhung cho di xa ma phan song moi bat dau hien ra.
EN: Every relationship is written in distance: near, far, staying, leaving, silence, waiting. This circle does not analyze emotion. It goes straight into the invisible structure between person and person. There are places close by that are not real. There are distances where the living part only begins to appear.

# Vong 6 / Circle 6 — Vai va mat / Role and face

VI: Con nguoi khong chi song. Con nguoi dien. Vong nay di vao lop mat na duoc deo lau den muc tuong la da thit that. Tin, nghi, xac nhan, thanh that, gia vo — tat ca khong con la khai niem dao duc. Chung la dau vet cua mot cau truc dang tu giu lay minh. Doc vong nay nhu soi guong nhung khong ninh minh.
EN: A human being does not only live. A human being performs. This circle enters the mask worn so long it is mistaken for real skin. Belief, doubt, confirmation, honesty, pretense — all are no longer moral concepts. They are traces of a structure holding onto itself. Read this circle as looking into a mirror without flattering yourself.

# Vong 7 / Circle 7 — Gia va so huu / Price and ownership

VI: Khong phai moi thu co gia deu co gia tri. Khong phai moi thu giu du deu dang giu. Vong nay di vao phan thi truong da chay sau vao cach con nguoi do chinh minh: du, thieu, giau, ngheo, no, hiem, duoc chon. Nhung cau o day khong day buong. Chung lam lo ra dieu gi dang dung sai cho trong cach ta goi ten gia tri.
EN: Not everything that has a price has value. Not everything that can be held is worth holding. This circle enters the part of the market that has flowed deep into how people measure themselves: enough, lacking, rich, poor, debt, rare, chosen. The sentences here do not teach letting go. They reveal what is standing in the wrong place in how we name value.

# Vong 8 / Circle 8 — Truoc khi lam / Before doing

VI: Truoc moi hanh dong lon luon co mot vung mo: chua chon, tri hoan, ne tranh, giao quyet dinh cho hoan canh, cho nguoi khac, cho thoi diem. Vong nay dung ngay truoc dong tac. Khong hoi thuc. Khong khuyen nhanh. Chi soi ra rang co nhung quyet dinh da nga tu rat som, con phan do du chi den de keo dai viec chua chiu nhan.
EN: Before every major action there is always a blurred zone: not yet chosen, postponed, avoided, handing the decision over to circumstance, to another person, to timing. This circle stands right before the gesture. No urging. No speeding up. It only reveals that some decisions had already leaned from very early on, while the hesitation only came to prolong what was not yet willing to admit itself.

# Vong 9 / Circle 9 — Phan con lai / What remains

VI: Sau tat ca, van con nhung dieu khong duoc noi, khong duoc dat ten, khong duoc giai quyet. Khong phai vi chung con thieu. Ma vi chung thuoc ve mot loai hieu khac. Vong cuoi khong tong ket. Khong ket luan. No chi de lai mot do im co hinh dang rieng: phan con lai cua cuon sach, va phan con lai cua nguoi doc.
EN: After everything, there are still things unsaid, unnamed, unresolved. Not because they are still lacking. But because they belong to a different kind of understanding. The final circle does not summarize. It does not conclude. It only leaves behind a stillness with its own shape: what remains of the book, and what remains of the reader.
